The 7s Lacrosse Approach

Structured Training.

Sevens Lacrosse is not built around random drills. Every session has a purpose. Athletes work on specific skills, learn why those skills matter, and build habits that carry into games.

Training sessions can include stick skills, shooting, passing, dodging, footwork, speed, agility, strength habits, lacrosse IQ. Sessions always include confidence building! Home practice is assigned between sessions.

Players can be evaluated through the proprietary 7s Player Progression System — a structured approach that helps athletes and parents understand current ability, areas for improvement, and the next step in development.

One-on-One

Private Lacrosse Training

One-on-one training for athletes who want focused coaching and individual attention.

Private sessions are built around the athlete's age, position, experience level, confidence, and goals. Training may include catching, throwing, cradling, shooting, dodging, footwork, stick protection, ground balls, off-ball movement, and position-specific skill work.

Private training is best for players who need detailed instruction, extra reps, confidence, or a clear development plan.

Most Complete Option

Lacrosse Performance Training

Focused on combining skill work with speed, agility, strength, conditioning, footwork, and basic nutrition education to support training.

This option is designed for athletes who need more than stick skills. Sessions can include movement prep, change of direction, balance, acceleration, deceleration, shooting mechanics, dodging footwork, lacrosse-specific athletic development, and simple fueling education.

Basic nutrition support is included only as general athlete education — not medical advice or a strict diet plan. The goal is to help athletes understand simple habits such as hydration, pre-training fueling basics, post-training recovery, and consistent eating around practices and games.

Lacrosse Performance Training is best for players who want to become stronger, faster, and more confident on the field.

Alex has been a Student-Athlete Advisory Committee Representative for 3 years while at Stockton University. As a SAAC Rep, Alex has acted as a vital bridge between the lacrosse team, fellow athletes, and the university's athletic administration.

Coach "Deli" lives and breathes lacrosse. His mission is straightforward: develop complete lacrosse athletes. Not only as highly skilled but athletes who are strong, and properly conditioned to play this very fast and physically demanding sport. Having played the sport for a long time and experienced many injuries, he believes his comprehensive training system will develop athletes who can play at a high level from their early years all the way through college and beyond.

The Sevens Lacrosse Performance System was developed by Alex DeLierre to develop athletes in a structured and personalized manner — not just random drills and exercises. Every milestone level and training session is designed so players — and their parents — can understand how they are developing, understand how they are being held accountable, and how the training they are doing applies to the sport.
