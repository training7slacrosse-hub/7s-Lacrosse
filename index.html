Who It's For

Built for Players Ages 8–18

7s Lacrosse is for elementary, middle school, and high school athletes who want more focused coaching than they get in large group practices. Training is available for beginners learning the game, experienced players sharpening their skills, and serious athletes looking for extra work outside of team practice.

Sessions are adjusted based on each athlete's age, experience level, position, confidence, and goals. The main focus is offensive skill development, stick work, footwork, athleticism, lacrosse IQ, and game-ready habits.

All positions are welcome. Attackmen and midfielders will receive the most position-specific offensive training. Defensemen, LSMs, goalies, and faceoff athletes can still benefit from general skill work, footwork, athletic development, passing, catching, shooting reps, and lacrosse IQ training, but full specialized position coaching for those roles is not currently offered.

7s Lacrosse trains both boys and girls. A female coach may also be available for athletes or families who prefer that option.

Coach

Alex DeLierre

Alex DeLierre is a senior Exercise Science major and Division III attackman at Stockton University. He has 15 years of competitive playing experience and brings a player-development approach to every 7s Lacrosse session.

In 2026, Alex earned USA Lacrosse Magazine All-America Honorable Mention, CLC First Team honors, and was named CLC Offensive Player of the Week twice. Playing attack, he finished the season with 57 goals, 62 points, 47 ground balls, and 13 caused turnovers — production that reflects scoring ability, riding effort, toughness, and all-around play.

A USA Lacrosse Bronze Certified Coach and CSC Academic All-District Honoree, Alex has volunteered at peewee clinics for seven years and provided private instruction for youth players for over a year. His goal with 7s Lacrosse is simple: give athletes clear, specific coaching instead of random drills. Every session is built so players know exactly what they are working on, why it matters, and how it applies to the game.

The Name

Why 7s?

The name 7s Lacrosse comes from the number 77. Alex has worn #77 since high school, and the number has become part of his identity as an athlete. Over time, it started to represent more than a jersey number.

The Belief

The 7s Standard

The 7s Standard is the belief that confidence comes from preparation. Athletes improve when they train with purpose, show up consistently, and learn how to do the work outside of practice.
